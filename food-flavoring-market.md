<p><p>The food flavoring market can be categorized into two main types: natural and synthetic. Natural flavorings are derived from plant or animal sources and are perceived as healthier and more authentic by consumers. They include extracts, essential oils, and essences. In contrast, synthetic flavorings are chemically manufactured to replicate specific tastes and aromas, offering consistency and cost-effectiveness. While natural flavors tend to have a higher market value due to their quality perception, synthetic flavors dominate in mass-produced food items for their affordability and stability.</p></p>
