<p><p>The Proton Exchange Membrane (PEM) fuel cell market features three key hydrogen storage types: compressed gaseous hydrogen, cryogenic liquid hydrogen, and hydrides. Compressed gaseous hydrogen is stored at high pressures, ensuring efficient transport and usage. Cryogenic liquid hydrogen offers high energy density by storing hydrogen at extremely low temperatures, maximizing storage efficiency. Hydrides involve chemical compounds that absorb hydrogen, providing a safer and more compact alternative. Each type has unique advantages, catering to various applications in the growing fuel cell market.</p></p>
