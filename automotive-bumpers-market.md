<p><p>The automotive bumper market is primarily divided into two types: metal and plastic bumpers. Metal bumpers, typically made from steel or aluminum, offer superior durability and impact resistance, often used in heavy-duty vehicles. However, they can be heavier and susceptible to corrosion. In contrast, plastic bumpers, commonly made from polypropylene or thermoplastic, are lightweight and versatile, allowing for complex designs and aesthetic options. They provide good impact absorption while enhancing fuel efficiency due to reduced vehicle weight.</p></p>
