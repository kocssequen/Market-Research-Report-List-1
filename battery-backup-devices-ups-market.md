<p><p>Battery backup devices, or Uninterruptible Power Supplies (UPS), provide emergency power during outages. The market features various battery types: Nickel-Cadmium (NiCd) batteries are known for their durability and reliability, particularly in demanding environments, but have environmental concerns. Lead-Acid batteries are cost-effective and widely used for backup systems, but they are heavier and less efficient. Lithium-Ion batteries offer higher energy density, longer life, and lighter weight, making them increasingly popular for modern UPS applications, despite their higher initial cost.</p></p>
